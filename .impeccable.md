# NOUS — Design Context

## Users
Product managers, developers, technical thinkers. Capture 10+ thoughts/day across contexts. Suffer "where did I write that?" retrieval friction. Want AI assist without losing authorial control. Value fluid beautiful UX equal to features.

Context of use: walking, in meetings, mid-thought, late at night, on commute. Mobile-first. Often one-handed. State of mind: mid-flow — must NEVER wait for network/spinner/AI to capture.

## Brand Personality
Three words: **cognitive · kinetic · understated**.

Voice: quiet confidence. No marketing language. No "AI ✨ magic". Treats user as smart. Speaks in fragments when fragments are enough. Reverent of the user's thought, not its own cleverness.

Emotional goals: feels like physical extension of mind. Spatial memory + kinetic gestures. Calm while idle, alive when touched.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Kinetic Minimalism** — retro-futurist Teenage Engineering DNA, dark phosphor CRT register, restrained for daily-tool ergonomics. Think OP-1 + nightside oscilloscope + Field Notes brain.

- Theme: **dark / black paper**. Deep near-black ground (oklch ~0.14, slight blue-violet tint, NOT pure #000). Neon phosphor accents: cyan, electric blue, CRT green, amber-yellow, hazard orange. Used SPARINGLY as atom-type markers, Orb glow, AI auras — accents are rare → powerful. ~10% visual weight max.
- Materiality: black paper + diffused phosphor glow + glass. AI states = `.ultraThinMaterial` membranes w/ ≥60pt Gaussian blur, ≤15% opacity colored aura behind. Never hard neon borders, never explicit "AI" labels. Glow = light bleeding through fog, not neon tube outline.
- Typography: smart, functional, slightly over-the-top yet practical. Distinctive display for day headers + system moments — explore industrial/scientific/instrument-panel faces (e.g. Söhne Mono / Berkeley Mono / Departure Mono / Reckless / Migra). Body = refined humanist, easy to read at length on dark. Mono for timestamps, system labels, atom metadata. Reject all reflex fonts (Inter, Fraunces, Playfair, Instrument Serif, IBM Plex, Space Grotesk, etc.).
- UX writing: smart, functional, a bit over-the-top yet practical. Voice = quiet operator, dry, occasional precision-flex. Never cute, never chatty, never apologetic. Empty/error states earn their words.
- The Orb: floating liquid heart of system. Metal/SwiftUI shader. Lives — slow ambient breathing at idle. Boils when voice captured (waveform → liquid deformation, color shifts w/ tone/loudness). Unique signature object — the one thing users will remember.

### Anti-references (forbidden)
- Notion / Linear / Things screenshots — too SaaS, too generic-card.
- Glassmorphism-everywhere look. Glass used purposefully for AI membrane only.
- Generic AI-app cyan→purple gradient backdrop, gradient text, side-stripe borders, rounded-rect drop shadows.
- Hard neon-tube outlines (Cyberpunk-77 register). Our neon = diffused phosphor glow through fog, not stroked SVG.
- Any "AI POLISH…" labels, sparkles, magic-wand icons.
- Folder/tag/hierarchy metaphors. No sidebars. No tabs.

## Design Principles
1. **Capture is sacred.** 0ms local write. Nothing — animation, network, AI — may delay the user's thought hitting storage.
2. **Aura over label.** AI presence shown through diffused color washes behind ultraThinMaterial, never through text or icons.
3. **Dual-state, never destructive.** Raw + refined coexist forever. Cross-fade reveals refinement; user can revert at will.
4. **Kinetic, not decorative.** Motion conveys causality (haptic ticks at month/year boundaries, orb boils on voice). No motion for "delight" alone.
5. **The Stream is the home.** Chronological atoms grouped by day, hand-lettered date headers, ink-dot type markers. No folders, no tags-as-navigation. Retrieval = type-to-search (semantic) or swipe-up Orb.
